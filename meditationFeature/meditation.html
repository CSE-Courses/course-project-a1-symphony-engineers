Meditation

Trancendental Meditation

Transcendental Meditation (commonly referred as TM) technique is a very simple, natural and effortless way of letting your mind settle down into an extremely calm and wise state of rest. Maharishi Mahesh Yogi created and introduced the TM technique and TM movement in India in the mid-1950s.

Benefits:

    Improves brain function and memory
    Greater sense of clarity and productivity
    Lowers blood pressure

Trancendental Meditation

Spiritual Meditation

Spiritual Meditation is the mindful practice of connection to something that is greater, vaster, and deeper than the individual self. It may seem paradoxical, but the path to that connection passes through honest self-reflection.

Benefits:

    Releases and settles our thoughts and emotions
    Relaxes your nervous system and helps your body unwind from stress
    Helps you to let go of the past and sink in peace

Spiritual Meditation

Mindful Meditation

Mindfulness meditation is a mental training practice that teaches you to slow down racing thoughts, let go of negativity, and calm both your mind and body. Mindfulness techniques can vary, but in general, mindfulness meditation involves a breathing practice and awareness of body and mind.

Benefits:

    Improves attention
    Helps make progress toward your weight-loss goals
    Decreases loneliness in seniors

Mindful Meditation

Focused Meditation

Focused meditation involves focusing on something intently as a way of staying in the present moment and slowing down the inner dialogue. In Focused meditation, you remain in the present, but focus wholly on one thing, typically sensory stimulus like sounds, visual items, tactile sensations, tastes, smells, and even your own breathing.

Benefits:

    Stress Relief
    Improved memory
    More self-awareness

Focused Meditation

Mantra Meditation

Mantra meditation is a method of rotation of consciousness around a sound, amplifying it for maximum effect. In the Yoga contemplative tradition, mantra meditation is often said to be the easiest and safest method.

Benefits:

    Increased self-compassion
    More positive outlook
    Improved mood and well-being
    Better control over breath

Mantra Meditation

Movement Meditation

Movement meditation focuses on the body in motion. Walking meditation is one form of mindful movement. This technique can also be associated with yoga or tai chi and other martial arts. Having a commitment to some form of physical discipline is very beneficial.

Benefits:

    Helps to reduce physical pain and boost the immune system.
    Encourages deep relaxation and feelings of peace
    Lowers blood pressure & increases blood flow to the brain
